<p><p>The non-reflective glass market primarily comprises two treatment types: chemical treatment and physical processing. Chemical treatment involves applying specialized coatings or treatments to the glass surface, enhancing its properties to minimize reflection and improve clarity. In contrast, physical processing techniques involve altering the glass structure through methods such as sandblasting or etching, which create a textured surface that reduces glare. Both approaches cater to diverse applications, including architectural, automotive, and display industries, aiming for enhanced visibility and aesthetic appeal.</p></p>
