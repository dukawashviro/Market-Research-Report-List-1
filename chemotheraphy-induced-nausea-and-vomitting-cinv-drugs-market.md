<p><p>The Chemotherapy Induced Nausea and Vomiting (CINV) drugs market is categorized into acute, delayed, breakthrough, and others. Acute CINV occurs within 24 hours post-chemotherapy and requires rapid intervention. Delayed CINV arises after the first 24 hours and can persist for several days, necessitating ongoing treatment. Breakthrough CINV refers to nausea and vomiting that occurs despite prophylactic measures, requiring rescue therapies. The "others" category includes various combinations and new treatment modalities aimed at alleviating CINV symptoms effectively.</p></p>
