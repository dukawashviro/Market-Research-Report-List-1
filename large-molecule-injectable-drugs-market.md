<p><p>The large molecule injectable drugs market encompasses various administration methods, including intravenous (IV), muscle (intramuscular), and subcutaneous injections. IV injections deliver drugs directly into the bloodstream, providing rapid effects, ideal for critical treatments. Muscle injections target deeper tissues, often used for vaccines and hormones, offering slower absorption. Subcutaneous injections, administered just under the skin, are suitable for self-administration and chronic conditions, enabling gradual drug release. Each method has specific applications based on the drug's formulation and desired therapeutic effect.</p></p>
