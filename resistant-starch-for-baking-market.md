<p><p>Resistant starch (RS) is a type of carbohydrate that resists digestion in the small intestine, emerging as a valuable ingredient in the baking market. RS1 is physically inaccessible starch found in whole grains and seeds. RS2 consists of ungelatinized starch from raw potatoes and green bananas. RS3 is formed when cooked and cooled starch retrogrades, like in pasta or rice. RS4 is chemically modified starch designed for specific functional properties. Each type offers health benefits and enhances texture in baked goods.</p></p>
