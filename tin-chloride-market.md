<p><p>The tin chloride market is primarily divided into two types: stannous chloride (SnCl2) and stannic chloride (SnCl4). Stannous chloride, known for its reducing properties, is widely used in electroplating, as a catalyst in chemical reactions, and in dyeing processes. Stannic chloride, on the other hand, acts primarily as a Lewis acid and finds applications in water treatment, pharmaceuticals, and as a catalyst in organic synthesis. Both types cater to diverse industries, influencing market dynamics and demand.</p></p>
