<p><p>The coagulation tests market is segmented based on technology types into mechanical, electrochemical, optical, and other methods. Mechanical methods use shear forces to assess coagulation, while electrochemical techniques measure changes in electrical signals during the clotting process. Optical methods rely on light scattering or absorbance to detect clot formation. Other technologies may include methods like microfluidics or biosensors. Each type offers distinct advantages for diagnosing clotting disorders, ensuring accurate and timely patient care in clinical settings.</p></p>
